No going upstairs for the hobbit: bedrooms, bathrooms,
cellars, pantries (lots of these), wardrobes (he had wholerooms devoted to clothes), kitchens, dining-rooms, allwere on the same floor, and indeed on the same passage.The best rooms were all on the left-hand side (going in),for these were the only ones to have windows, deep-set round windows looking over his garden, and meadows beyond, sloping down to the river.

This hobbit was a very well-to-do hobbit, and his
name was Baggins. The Bagginses had lived in the neigh-
bourhood of The Hill for time out of mind, and people
considered them very respectable, not only because most
of them were rich, but also because they never had any
adventures or did anything unexpected: you could tell
what a Baggins would say on any question without the
bother of asking him. This is a story of how a Baggins
had an adventure, and found himself doing and saying
things altogether unexpected. He may have lost the
neighbours’ respect, but he gained—well, you will see
whether he gained anything in the end.
